Mental Health

The state of your mental health affects your wellbeing and quality of life. We're here to present a few options for managing your mental health.

"There is hope even when your brain tells you there isn't".

- John Green

Why Your Mental Health is Important

Mental health covers a large umbrella of emotions people feel as a result of internal and external circumstances. Mental health issues don't stem from the emotions you feel but rather how you manage and express these emotions.

How was your day?

Communicating your emotions is a good way to manage your mental health. 

Talking with Friends 

It's easy to get caught up in your thoughts. Expressing your emotions to someone you know well can help you articulate how you feel. You could also ask your friend for their input on your situation if you're looking for a new perpective. 

Talking with Family

Your family can be another good outlet for communicating your emotions. Choose a family member you feel you could tell almost anything to and trust that they are there to help you get through this. 

Talking to a Therapist

If the distress you're feeling is taking up the majority of your thoughts and you can tell your quality of life is decreasing you should consider professional help. Click the link below to get in contact with a therapist in St. Louis.

Get your blood pumping

Exercise is another great way to improve your mood when you're not at your best.

Exercise and Mood

Exercise increase the levels of endorphines your body produces which naturally improves your mood. Exercise also reduces chemicals produces in your immune system that make depression worse. Consider taking a walk or working out to help lift your mood.
